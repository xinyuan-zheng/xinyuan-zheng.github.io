Hi, nice to meet you!

I am a postgraduate research associate at Blumenfeld Lab, Yale Clinical Neurosciences Imaging Center (CNIC), Yale School of Medicine.

My current projects focus on machine learning and statistical analysis for MRI and EEG datasets.

I love mathematics and statistics (and modelling and algorithm)! I study and work on image analysis related projects. I am especially interested in medical/clincal research, as I feel these research can directly help people, and that keeps me motivated. I am currently working on epilepsy related projects using data collected from animal models (rats and mice).

I use python for most of my projects, R for statistical research, and MATLAB for neuroimaging related research. As you can tell, by building this website I am also learning a bit of html/CSS!


More about me

I obtained my bachelor's degree in Mathematics with Finance at University of Liverpool. I enjoyed my time in Liverpool. I met great teachers and friends, learnt a lot about applied mathematics, and did a lot of sketches of the beautiful architectures there.

I came to New York in 2019 and graduated from Columbia University this year, with a master's in Statistics. I studied Inference, Bayesian and Machine Learning. I did not have the opportunity to explore the city due to the pandemic...

During my time at Columbia, I did deep learning and medical imaging related research with Dr. Upmanu Lall and Dr. Xiaofu He.

I joined Blumenfeld lab at Clinical Neuroscience Imaging Center, Yale School of Medicine after my graduation, and I currently work with Dr. Blumenfeld Hal, Dr. Cian McCafferty and Dr. Lim-Anna Sieu, focusing on the development of analysis methods for fMRI.
